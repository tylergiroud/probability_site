The State of AI Search

March 2026

Search is being rewritten. Not incrementally, not as a feature update, but fundamentally. The way people discover information, products, and brands is shifting from a list of blue links to a conversation.

ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and others are becoming the first place people go for answers. And unlike traditional search, these models don't return ten results. They return one answer, maybe two. If your brand isn't in that answer, you don't exist.

What this means for brands

The playbook that worked for SEO doesn't directly translate to AI visibility. You can't just optimize a page title and meta description and hope for the best. AI models synthesize information from across the web, weighting trust, recency, and the quality of the source.

This is exactly the problem we're solving at Lectern. Brands need to show up in the training data, in the citations, in the sources that AI models trust.

The opportunity

For brands that move early, the opportunity is massive. AI search is still in its infancy. The companies that build visibility now will have a compounding advantage as these models become the default way people find things.

The best time to build AI visibility was yesterday. The second best time is now.

We're still in the early innings. But the game has already changed.
